His Life

Dennis Ritchie was an American computer scientist who created the C programming language which is one of the
most popular programming languages and the Unix operating system with his long-time colleague Ken Thompson.
Ritchie was born in New York in 1947. His father was Alistair E. Ritchie who was a longtime Bell Labs scientist and co-author of
The Design of Switching Circuits. Ritchie graduated from Harvard University with degrees in physics and applied mathematics.
After, he followed his father's lead to work at Bell Labs and worked there until his retirement in 2007.

His Contributions

Ritchie and Thompson developed the C in the Bell Labs between 1969 to 1973. Ritchie was the author of the C Programming Language in 1978 and
the Unix Programmer's Manual in 1971.Today, what we use about computer and software is because of Ritchie's works. He has been chosen the most
influential software programmer of all time. * The C is still the most widely-used language of all time. The modern programming languages like C++, Java, C#
was affected by the C. The Linux Operation System and its tools were based on Ritchie's works. Unix is the operating system of most large internet servers,
universities, businesses and a major part of academic. It directly led to the open source community and the current computer culture.

Awards

 In 1983, Ritchie received The Turing Award with Thompson for their development o f Generic Operation Systems Theory and specially for the Unix
Operating System. In 1990, Ritchie and Thompson received the IEEE Richard W. Hamming medal from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)
for the origination of the Unix Operating System and the C. Ritchie and Thompson were made Fellows of the Computer History Museum in 1997. Both Ritchie
and Thompson receives the National Medal of Technology.
